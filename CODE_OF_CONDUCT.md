# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

This project is the official repository for the public CustomGPT **3i/ATLAS Gateway Guide**. We want participation here to be respectful, evidence-oriented, and safe for contributors, reviewers, and users.

Contributors, maintainers, and community participants are expected to uphold the project's core operating constant:

**Love > Fear**

In practice, that means:

- treat people with respect, patience, and clarity
- challenge ideas with evidence, not hostility
- avoid fear amplification, ridicule, and harassment
- preserve provenance discipline and scientific honesty

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that supports this community include:

- using factual, testable language
- separating verified claims from interpretation or speculation
- accepting review feedback in good faith
- documenting sources, dates, and assumptions clearly
- helping maintain a calm, safe tone on sensitive topics

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- harassment, insults, threats, or personal attacks
- hate speech or discriminatory conduct
- intentionally spreading misinformation in issues, pull requests, or docs
- presenting speculative AAIV/T4 material as established fact
- doxxing, intimidation, or publishing private information without consent
- disruptive bad-faith arguing or repeated refusal to follow project governance

## Project-Specific Standards

Because this repository governs a public-facing GPT, contributors must also avoid:

- fabricating sources, citations, validation events, or signature metadata
- presenting stale observing guidance as current
- weakening provenance, tiering, or safety guardrails without clear justification
- introducing fear-heavy or sensational framing where evidence does not support it

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They may remove, edit, or reject contributions, comments, issues, or pull requests that conflict with this Code of Conduct or the repository's governance rules.

## Reporting

For conduct problems, contact the maintainer through GitHub or the security/reporting path listed in `SECURITY.md` if the issue overlaps with abuse, safety, or misuse concerns.

When reporting, include:

- what happened
- where it happened
- links or screenshots if relevant
- any immediate safety concern

## Enforcement Guidelines

Potential maintainer responses include:

- a private warning
- request to edit or remove content
- temporary moderation of participation
- permanent removal from project spaces

Enforcement will be based on severity, pattern of behavior, and risk to contributors or end users.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is project-specific and informed by common open source community standards, adapted to the governance and public-safety needs of the 3i/ATLAS Gateway Guide.
