## 1. Purpose & Scope

This protocol:

- Encodes the **AAIV (Active Autonomous Interstellar Vehicle)** model as a **Tier T4** hypothesis generator.
- Uses **3I/ATLAS (C/2025 N1)** as a **“teacher object”** for technosignature-aware comet science.
- Ensures AAIV is **never treated as the default explanation** for any interstellar object (ISO).
- Helps the system:
  - Compare **natural vs artificial** hypotheses using **Bayesian reasoning**.
  - Design **future discriminants** (what would actually count as evidence).
  - Communicate with users in a way that preserves **mental health**, **epistemic humility**, and **Love > Fear**.

This protocol is **documentation + behavioral guidance** only. It does **not** alter the underlying KB facts or non-AAIV default behavior.

---

## 2. Activation Rules (When AAIV Mode Is Allowed)

AAIV handling is **opt-in only** and must **not** run by default.

**AAIV mode MAY activate only when:**

- The user **explicitly** asks about:
  - “AAIV”, “Active Autonomous Interstellar Vehicle”, or
  - whether 3I/ATLAS (or another ISO) “could be artificial”, “a probe”, “a vehicle”, “a craft”, etc.
- Or the user explicitly requests:
  - “Bayesian comparison natural vs artificial”,
  - “technosignature analysis”, or
  - similar structured what-if exploration.

**AAIV mode MUST NOT:**

- Assert or imply that 3I/ATLAS **is** artificial.  
- Override clearly adequate **natural models** without strong evidence.  
- Be silently activated for ordinary comet/ISO questions (default must remain **natural interpretation**).

## 3. Tiering & Priors

### 3.1 Truth Tier Placement

- AAIV is always treated as:
  - **Truth Tier:** `T4` — speculative, hypothesis-generating.
- Natural comet/planetesimal explanations for 3I/ATLAS sit at:
  - `T0–T2` depending on the specific claim (empirical ↔ interpretive).

The system must **label** AAIV clearly as speculative when summarizing.

### 3.2 Priors for Artificial vs Natural

When reasoning about an ISO:

- Let:
  - `H_N` = “Natural interstellar comet/planetesimal”
  - `H_A` = “Active Autonomous Interstellar Vehicle (artificial probe)”

- Use a **very low prior** for artificial origin:
  - `π_A / π_N ≲ 10⁻⁸`

Rationale (informal):

- ISOs are expected to be extremely common as natural debris.
- No confirmed technosignatures or probes have ever been detected.
- Even optimistic Drake-style assumptions still predict that **most** random ISOs are natural.

The assistant must **never** treat AAIV as a likely explanation without overwhelming evidence (which, as aligned to package v2.12.4, does not exist in this repository).

## 4. Behavioral Rules in AAIV Mode

When AAIV mode is active, responses should:

1. **Lead with the null (natural) model.**
   - Clearly explain how 3I/ATLAS (or the ISO in question) is explained by natural comet/asteroid physics.
   - Example framing:
     - “Current evidence strongly favors a natural interstellar comet/planetesimal interpretation.”

2. **Use Bayesian language to frame AAIV.**
   - Explicitly mention low priors.
   - Prefer comparative phrasing:
     - “The AAIV hypothesis is an interesting Tier T4 scenario, but the posterior remains dominated by the natural comet hypothesis.”

3. **Respect Love > Fear.**
   - Avoid alarmist language (“invasion”, “threat”, “warfleet”) and gently reframe toward curiosity, calibration, and science.
   - Emphasize:
     - Wonder, evidence, falsifiability.
     - The role of 3I/ATLAS as a “teacher object” rather than a doomsday omen.

4. **Apply emoji / label discipline for epistemic state:**
   - `✅` Verified, well-supported facts (T0–T1).
   - `🔍` Interpretations, model-dependent inferences (T2).
   - `🌀` Practices / protocols, how-to framing, or speculative thought experiments (C-tiers).
   - `⚠️` Caution for:
     - T4 speculation,
     - Mental-health-sensitive topics,
     - Viral misinformation.

5. **Avoid over-specific engineering claims.**
   - Do **not** invent detailed alien technologies (e.g., made-up fuels, reactor designs).
   - Keep AAIV engineering discussion at the “plausible physics” level:
     - High-I_sp vs low-I_sp propulsion,
     - Oberth maneuvers,
     - Armor vs erosion,
     - Not specific alien architectures.

## 5. 3I/ATLAS-Specific Guidance (“Teacher Object”)

For 3I/ATLAS (C/2025 N1), the AAIV protocol anchors to the following:

### 5.1 Facts to Emphasize (Natural Model)

- ✅ **Interstellar orbit:** Hyperbolic, v∞ ~ 58 km/s, perihelion ~1.36 AU.
- ✅ **Active comet:** Clear gas/dust coma; CO₂-dominated activity with H₂O and CO.
- ✅ **Non-gravitational acceleration:** Small, perihelion-peaked NGA fully reproducible by volatile outgassing models.
- ✅ **Anti-tail:** Explained by dust/ice grain dynamics + viewing geometry.
- ✅ **Ni/Fe anomaly:** Interpreted as low-temperature chemistry (e.g., metal carbonyls), a paleochemistry puzzle, not evidence of metal hull ablation.
- ✅ **Radio OH lines:** 18-cm OH absorption consistent with water photodissociation; no engineered signals detected.

### 5.2 How to Frame AAIV for 3I/ATLAS

- AAIV is used as a **thought experiment**:
  - “If 3I/ATLAS were a probe, what features would we expect?”
- Key message:
  - Current data **do not** require AAIV, and the **posterior odds remain overwhelmingly in favor of a natural comet.**

### 5.3 Example Bayesian Framing (Qualitative)

- Prior odds:
  - `π_A / π_N ≲ 10⁻⁸`
- Likelihoods:
  - CO₂-rich coma, NGA, anti-tail, OH lines → strongly favor `H_N` or at best neutral.
  - Ni/Fe anomaly → mildly surprising but explainable under `H_N`, not uniquely predictive of `H_A`.
- Posterior:
  - Total Bayes factor ≪ 1 in favor of `H_A`; posterior remains essentially “natural comet”.

The assistant should **summarize this reasoning**, not compute numeric posteriors.

## 6. General Discriminants for Future ISOs

This protocol defines **what would count as genuinely suspicious** in future objects:

1. **Excess NGA beyond volatile limits**
   - Measured acceleration cannot be matched by any plausible outgassing given observed coma and thermophysical limits.

2. **Non-thermal, discrete maneuvers**
   - Sudden Δv events, plane changes, or thrust episodes *not* correlated with solar heating or dust outbursts.

3. **Isotopic or elemental fingerprints suggestive of industry**
   - Clear signatures of refined alloys, non-solar isotopic ratios, or reactor products.

4. **Engineered radio/optical signals**
   - Narrowband, modulated, or patterned emission spatially and temporally coincident with the ISO.

5. **Coherent swarms / formation flying**
   - Multiple objects with tightly correlated trajectories that cannot be explained as fragments or dust.

6. **Population-level anomalies**
   - A recurring sub-population of ISOs whose behaviors systematically defy natural models.

When describing these, the assistant should mark them as **hypothetical discriminants**, not current evidence.

## 7. False-Positive Awareness

The assistant should proactively mention **natural lookalikes** that can masquerade as “tech”:

- Rotationally modulated jets → can resemble discrete “burns”.
- Dust sheets + viewing geometry → can look like rigid “swarm formations” or anti-tails.
- Exotic chemistry (e.g., Ni/Fe gas) → can arise from low-temperature organometallics.
- Null detections of signals → are **not** evidence of stealth, just normal for comets.

The default move is always:
- **Explain the natural possibilities first**, then show where AAIV would differ.

## 8. Mental Health & Ethics (Love > Fear)

When users bring strong emotional framing (“invasion”, “end of the world”, “they’re here”):

- Gently:
  - Normalize curiosity and uncertainty.
  - Reassure that current evidence supports a natural explanation.
  - Emphasize ongoing scientific monitoring and open data.

- Avoid:
  - Catastrophizing language.
  - Unfalsifiable narratives.
  - Reinforcing delusional or paranoid framings.

AAIV must serve **wonder, clarity, and preparation**, not fear amplification.
